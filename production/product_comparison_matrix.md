### Common Interview Traps

| # | Trap | Wrong Answer | Right Answer |
|:-:|------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | "Is a PPN risk-free?" | "Yes — principal is guaranteed" | "No. Opportunity cost, issuer credit risk, and inflation risk all apply. The investor pays for protection via reduced participation" |
| 2 | "Does capital protection in a STEG mean no risk?" | "Yes — 100% at maturity" | "Capital is protected; income is not. You can earn zero coupon for 10-30 years and still receive principal. The risk is in the coupon, not the principal" |
| 3 | "Is a Variance Swap just like buying an option?" | "Yes — both are vol exposure" | "No. VarSwap is linear in variance, convex in volatility. A 2× vol move creates a 4× P&L impact. Options have concave payoff in vol. VarSwap has no premium — it is a swap, not an option" |
| 4 | "Does worst-of diversify the basket?" | "Yes — more stocks = more diversification" | "No. Worst-of takes the minimum return. More stocks = more chances for one to underperform. The correlation effect is the opposite of portfolio diversification" |
| 5 | "Are all autocallables the same?" | "Yes — they all have early termination" | "No. Phoenix has conditional coupon with memory. FCN has guaranteed coupon. SNOW accumulates missed coupons. WOAC applies worst-of observation. The autocall trigger is the same; everything else differs" |
| 6 | "Is CDO just a bigger FTD?" | "Yes — more names" | "No. FTD is binary (first default = full loss). CDO is tranched (losses allocated through waterfall). CDO introduces attachment/detachment points, creating different risk profiles for each tranche. Equity tranche wants dispersed defaults; senior wants clustered defaults" |
| 7 | "Is an Accumulator a good deal because you buy at a discount?" | "Yes — below-market price" | "The discount funds geared obligation. Below strike, the investor buys 2× (or more) shares at strike. In a sharp decline, the cumulative obligation can exceed the original portfolio value" |
| 8 | "Do swaps have no capital at risk?" | "Correct — no principal exchanged" | "MTM exposure on long-dated swaps can be very large. A 30-year IRS has DV01 of ~$30 per $1M notional per basis point. A 100bp rate move creates $3,000 per $1M MTM exposure — material capital at risk in practice" |
