## 5. Professor Voice Consistency

### Voice Markers

| Marker | Count | Avg/Chapter | Assessment |
|--------|:-----:|:-----------:|-----------|
| Direct address ("you") | 528 | 10.8 | Present but uneven |
| Question marks | 824 | 16.8 | Socratic style present |
| Imperative openings | 15 | 0.3 | Very rare |
| Plain language markers | 30 | 0.6 | Low — relies on analogy |

### Two Distinct Voice Profiles

**Profile A — "Conversational professor" (Batches 0-3, 9-10):**
- Multi-paragraph §1 with story opening
- Extended analogies with explicit mapping
- High direct-address count (12-20 "you" per chapter)
- Scenario-heavy §10 with numbered scenarios

**Profile B — "Dense technical narrator" (Batches 6-7):**
- Single-paragraph §1 with character-driven vignette
- Compressed analogy in 1-2 paragraphs
- Moderate direct-address (6-15 per chapter)
- Table-heavy §10 with fewer narrative scenarios

**Profile C — "Structured explainer" (Batches 4-5, 8):**
- Definition-first §1
- Moderate analogy depth
- Lower direct-address (3-7 per chapter)
- Balanced table/narrative mix

**Recommendation for Pass B:** Align toward Profile A (conversational professor) as it scores highest on accessibility metrics while maintaining technical accuracy.
