### Missing Format: Phone Screen

Phone screens at bulge bracket banks typically test:
1. "What is a reverse convertible?" (30 seconds — Tier 1 elevator pitch)
2. "Name 3 risks" (factual recall)
3. "Why structured products?" (motivation)
4. "Walk me through a payoff diagram" (visual communication over phone)

The Interview System's Tier 1 answers serve this purpose but no dedicated phone-screen practice set exists.

### Missing Format: Super Day Simulation

No guidance on managing energy, consistency, and depth variation across 4-6 consecutive interviews. Important for senior roles.

## 5. Trap Realism

### Technical Traps (T.1-T.20)

All 20 technical traps are realistic and well-constructed. Examples:

| Trap | Common Naive Answer | Correct Answer | Would Bank Ask? |
|------|:------------------:|:--------------:|:---------------:|
| T.4 (Vol ≠ risk) | "Higher vol = riskier" | Vol is one dimension; correlation, liquidity, model risk matter | YES — all banks |
| T.11 (Var strike) | "VarSwap has fixed strike" | Strike is in vol²; P&L is in realized vol² minus strike² | YES — GS, MS, Citi |
| T.13 (Smile) | "Vol is constant" | Skew/smile exists; causes mispricing if ignored | YES — all banks |
| T.16 (Phoenix ≠ RC) | "They're the same" | Different barrier types, observation, coupon memory | YES — all banks |

### Behavioural Traps (BT.1-BT.10)

All 10 behavioural traps are realistic. BT.7 (self-awareness) and BT.9 (client management) are particularly strong.

### Missing Infrastructure Traps

10 infrastructure traps identified in gap analysis (H-6). All would be asked at bulge bracket banks:

- "Modified Following and Following are the same" — asked at every European bank
- "Day count doesn't matter" — asked at SG, BNP, DB
- "P&L Explain residual is noise" — asked at every bank for PC/Trading
- "CSA threshold = no collateral" — asked at every bank with OTC desk

## 6. Answer Framework Realism

### 4-Tier Framework Assessment

| Tier | Name | Duration | Format | Realistic? |
|:----:|------|:--------:|--------|:----------:|
| 1 | Elevator | 30s | One-line definition | YES — phone screen answer |
| 2 | Technical | 2 min | Construction + Greeks + risk | YES — first round answer |
| 3 | Desk | 5 min | Hedging + systems + lifecycle | YES — desk round answer |
| 4 | Senior | 10 min | Failure modes + regulation + cross-functional | YES — senior round answer |

The 4-tier framework is the Interview System's strongest structural innovation. It maps precisely to how real interviews escalate depth.

### Framework Gap

The tiers escalate within product knowledge only. A parallel infrastructure tier system would be:

| Tier | Product (existing) | Infrastructure (missing) |
|:----:|:------------------:|:------------------------:|
| 1 | "What is an RC?" | "What is P&L Explain?" |
| 2 | "How is an RC constructed?" | "Walk through the P&L explain process" |
| 3 | "How do you hedge an RC?" | "You have $500K unexplained. What's your investigation?" |
| 4 | "Lehman 2008 — what happened?" | "IPV shows a $2M difference. Reserve governance says..." |
