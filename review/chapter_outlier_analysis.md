## Methodology

Composite score (1-10) calculated from six weighted dimensions using **word-count analysis** across key educational sections. This method corrects for formatting differences (dense paragraphs vs multi-paragraph) that inflate/deflate line-count metrics.

| Dimension | Weight | Source Section |
|-----------|:------:|:-------------:|
| Analogy Quality | 20% | §1 + §2 word count |
| Beginner Accessibility | 15% | §1 word count |
| Worked Example Depth | 20% | §18 word count |
| Knowledge Check Quality | 15% | §19 word count |
| Desk Reality Depth | 15% | §14 word count |
| Scenario Coverage | 15% | §10 word count |

### What Makes Weak Chapters Weak

**Pattern 1: SRT/STEG Compression (7 of bottom 10)**
- Batch 6 (SRT) and Batch 7 (STEG) chapters consistently have:
  - §1 under 140 words (vs 183 avg)
  - §2 under 100 words (vs 152 avg)
  - §14 under 100 words (vs 136 avg)
- Root cause: These were generated with a template that emphasised technical precision over pedagogical depth. The content is accurate but educationally compressed.

**Pattern 2: Thin Scenarios (ELO outlier)**
- 5.6.4 ELO has only 37 words in §10 — a severe outlier (next lowest is 93 words). This chapter needs scenario expansion regardless of other dimensions.

**Pattern 3: Swap Chapter Thinness (2 of bottom 10)**
- Equity Swap and Vanilla Swap have thin §14 (desk reality) sections. Swaps are structurally simpler than notes, which partly justifies thinner treatment, but 65-66 words is below acceptable minimum.
