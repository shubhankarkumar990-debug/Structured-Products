Structured Products — Start Here

The complete learning and reference library for structured products: one teaching Bible and five companions.

This page is the front door. It tells you what each document is, who it is for, and when to open it. Every link below works in the digital (HTML) edition; in print, each document is self-contained.

The library at a glance

# Document What it is Reach for it when…
0 The Desk Bible The main volume — Parts 0–7, 49 product chapters, dual-lens (investor + bank), 174 diagrams, worked examples. You want to learn a product or concept properly.
1 Solutions Manual A structurer’s decision framework + a scenario library (client want → product choice → why). You want applied decision practice.
2 Product Comparison Matrices Side-by-side tables + decision trees distinguishing similar products. You need to tell two products apart fast.
3 Product DNA Atlas One reference card per product (payoff, risks, system, lifecycle). You want a quick flip-card recap.
4 Interview System Graded interview Q&A, traps, model answers. You are preparing for interviews.
5 Market Infrastructure Encyclopedia Termsheet fields, market conventions, operational terms. You are reading a real termsheet or booking screen.

Quick start by role

  • New joiner, no finance background → start at the Bible, Part 0, and read forward. Keep the DNA Atlas open for recap.
  • New joiner, finance background → Bible Part 1 → Part 5. Use the Comparison Matrices to separate look-alike products.
  • Product control / operations → Bible Part 6 + the Infrastructure Encyclopedia.
  • Trader / structurer → Bible Part 5 + the Solutions Manual scenario library.
  • Interview prep → Bible product chapters → the Interview System → the Comparison Matrices for confusion pairs.

How the library is wired

Every numbered section and Part in the Bible has a stable anchor — #part-6 for Operations, #sec-5-1-1 for Reverse Convertibles, and so on. References in the text ([§5.1.1](Desk_Bible_v2.html#sec-5-1-1)) are clickable and jump straight to the target. An automated link-integrity check runs in the build, so these links cannot silently rot.

Two editions: the HTML edition is searchable and cross-linked across every document — best for daily work. The print PDF/DOCX editions are self-contained for offline reading. Same content; only navigation differs.
